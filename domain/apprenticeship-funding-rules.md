# Apprenticeship Funding Rules

**Source:** https://www.gov.uk/guidance/apprenticeship-funding-rules
**Published by:** Department for Education
**Last updated:** 15 May 2025

## What They Are

The funding rules set out the conditions that **employers** and **training providers** must meet in order to claim government funding for apprenticeship training and assessment in England. Different rules apply depending on when the apprenticeship **started** — the relevant rules are always those in force at the apprentice's start date.

## Provider Types

Funding rules distinguish between:

- **Employers** — organisations hiring and funding apprentices directly
- **Employer-providers** — employers who also deliver the training themselves
- **Main providers** — independent training providers (ITPs), colleges, universities

---

## Key Obligations (Common Themes Across Years)

- Apprentice must be in genuine employment and paid at least minimum wage
- Off-the-job (OTJ) training must meet the required threshold
- Training must be to an approved apprenticeship standard or framework
- Provider must submit accurate ILR data (see [ilr.md](ilr.md))
- Subcontracting arrangements must be declared and approved
