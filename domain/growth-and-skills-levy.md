# Growth and Skills Levy

**Source:** https://find-employer-schemes.education.gov.uk/interim/growth-and-skills-levy

## What It Is

The Growth and Skills Levy is a UK government initiative that builds on and reforms the apprenticeship levy. Its aim is to give employers more flexibility in how levy funds can be used — beyond traditional apprenticeships — to develop relevant, high-quality skills aligned to economic priorities.

---

## Products Under the Levy

### 1. Apprenticeships (existing)
Traditional earn-while-you-learn programmes tied to specific occupational standards. Unchanged in concept but subject to policy reforms below.

### 2. Foundation Apprenticeships (new)
- Target group: young people aged **16–24**
- Entry-level positions in: construction, engineering, health & social care, digital
- From **April 2026**: catering and retail sectors added
- Employer support: up to **£2,000** in support costs

### 3. Apprenticeship Units (launching April 2026)
- Short, flexible courses: **30–140 hours**
- For **employed workers aged 19+**
- Initial subjects: AI leadership, battery manufacturing, electric vehicle charging, solar installation, welding
- Designed for upskilling/reskilling without committing to a full apprenticeship

## Key Policy Changes

### Levy Fund Mechanics (from 1 August 2026)
| Change | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Government top-up | 10% top-up on levy funds **ends** |
| Fund expiry | New funds expire after **12 months** (was 24 months) |
| Co-investment | Employer contribution rises from **5% → 25%** |

### SME Financial Support
| From | Change |
|------|--------|
| 2026–27 | SMEs receive **fully-funded** apprenticeships for workers aged under 25 |
| October 2027 | Non-levy payers receive up to **£2,000 incentive** when hiring apprentices aged 16–24 |

### Level 7 Apprenticeships (from January 2026)
Government funding restricted to:
- Those aged **16–21**, OR
- Under 25 if they are **care leavers** or hold an **EHCP** (Education, Health and Care Plan)

### Streamlined Standards (from September 2026)
- Government funding withdrawn from **16 apprenticeship standards**
- Includes 3 leadership/management apprenticeships and 13 others deemed misaligned to critical skills or insufficiently suited for young people

### English & Maths Flexibility
- Adults aged **19+** no longer required to obtain formal English/maths qualifications
- Competency evidenced through application in real work tasks instead

### Minimum Duration
- Apprenticeships can now complete in **8 months** (was 12) where prior learning is formally recognised

## Levy Mechanics (Background)

- Employers with annual pay bill **> £3 million** pay **0.5%** of their total pay bill as the levy
- Funds sit in employer DAS (Digital Apprenticeship Service) accounts and can be used to pay for training/assessment
- Annual apprenticeship budget is set by HM Treasury and managed by DfE — it is **separate** from levy receipts
- **2023/24 budget split:** £2,525m total
  - 69.7% → levy-paying employers
  - 27.5% → non-levy employers
  - 2% → programme support

---

## Assessment Model Changes

- Assessment processes being streamlined for flexibility
- Training providers can now deliver some assessment elements
- End-Point Assessment Organisations (EPAOs) remain responsible for ensuring assessment validity
